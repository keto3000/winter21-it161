Social Media Optimization (SMO)

The advent of sophisticated social media algorithms has caused major shifts in the way that websites choose to advertise and establish their brands. Social Media Optimization (SMO) is fast becoming a mainstay marketing strategy. Traditionally, most online small businesses have underutilized social media marketing in favor of the ‘point-to-site’ link style of online branding mainly due to limited resources. Today, more than 60% of small businesses favor social media marketing and ~20% more say they plan to invest in SMO in the future(McKeon).

Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Pinterest, LinkedIn and other social media giants are actively moving to discourage the overused ‘click-baiting’ (‘like this’ and ‘tag this’) technique which marketers have used for years to game the search engine algorithms. Public frustration with link-heavy advertising, often considered spam, has become a definite turnoff for potential customers (Barnhart).

Many brands are revamping their websites to feature more personalized and entertaining contents— discussions, text questions, storytelling, videos and interactive graphics, such as carousel ads to excite, stimulate and engage the website user. Brant Barnhart, at SproutSocial.com, explains, “…people are sick of spam and impersonal brands alike. This is why storytelling content is all-the-rage right now. Oftentimes lacking any sort of concrete CTA or accompanying link, story-based posts are meant to drive discussion rather than clicks (Barnhart).” Content-driven searches expand audiences across social media platforms. They have a higher chance of getting into your followers’ feeds translating into increased public exposure, new followers, and ultimately increased business.

Maintenance of a brand’s marketing edge and public relevance on social media sites requires continual upkeep- regular posts, responses, and daily vigilance are a must.

One cost-effective strategy to optimize social media marketing is to transform some of your website’s ‘dry’ text content, like statistics, quotes, etc. into engaging media and images to convey the same information in a fun way. Visitors love to share pictures found while surfing the net!

The use of ‘tactical tagging’ can definitely give your brand a marketing edge. Tagging big influencers within your industry, specific market audiences, and use of familiar hashtags can greatly increase your brand’s visibility (Sana).

Another great way to optimize your brand’s social media presence is through the use of sharing and following plugin buttons on your website. Caroline Forsey, manager of Hubspot’s Marketing Blog, provides a nice how to guide on this topic for six of the major social media platforms and explains how to embed your social profile on your website to greatly enhance brand visibility (Forsey).
